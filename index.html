Thursday · 2 April 2026

First Coffee,
Heritage & Hawkers

Ease into Melaka the right way: a third-wave espresso in a restored Peranakan shophouse, then a slow drift through Dutch squares and colonial ruins, with chicken rice balls and satay celup waiting at the end.

Jonker Street · Melaka
☀️
Morning
Arrival
Into Melaka
Arrive from Singapore (~4 hours by bus). Check in at the heritage zone. The Majestic Malacca or Casa del Rio sit perfectly within walking distance of everything.
Check-in
9:00 am
Daily Fix Cafe ☕
Melaka's most beloved heritage cafe on Jalan Hang Jebat. A beautifully restored Peranakan shophouse serving specialty espresso, eggs Benedict on Nyonya toast, and housemade kaya jam. Expect a queue; worth every minute.
Cafe
The Daily Fix · 55 Jalan Hang Jebat
10:30 am
Jonker Street Morning Walk
Before the afternoon crowds, Jonker Street belongs to locals. Pick up fresh Nyonya kueh: butterfly blue rice cakes, ondeh-ondeh and kueh talam, from the morning kueh ladies.
Street Food
🌤️
Afternoon
12:00 pm
Chung Wah: Chicken Rice Balls 🍚
Melaka's most iconic dish: fragrant Hainanese chicken rice hand-rolled into golf-ball sized orbs. Chung Wah on Jalan Hang Jebat has been serving these since the 1960s. Queue before noon or wait 45 minutes.
Must-Eat
Chung Wah · Hainanese Chicken Rice Balls since the 1960s
1:30 pm
Baba & Nyonya Heritage Museum
Step inside a perfectly preserved Peranakan townhouse at 48–50 Jalan Tun Tan Cheng Lock. Intricate porcelain, carved furniture and family heirlooms tell the story of Straits Chinese culture.
Culture
3:00 pm
Calanthe Art Cafe ☕
A gorgeous cafe concept built around all 13 Malaysian states, each with its own signature coffee blend. Order the Melaka kopi with gula melaka syrup and sit in the cool vintage interior of this 1920s shophouse on Jalan Hang Kasturi.
Cafe
Calanthe Art Cafe · 13 states of Malaysia, one coffee each
4:00 pm
Christ Church, Stadthuys & Dutch Square
The iconic blood-red Dutch colonial square: Christ Church (1753), the Stadthuys administrative building, and the Dutch fountain. Golden hour light here is extraordinary for photography.
Landmark
5:00 pm
St. Paul's Hill at Dusk
Climb the cobbled path to the Portuguese church ruins of 1521. Watch the city light up across the Straits of Melaka from the hilltop as the day cools.
Views
🌙
Evening
6:30 pm
Sunset River Cruise
A 30-minute glide along the Melaka River past painted heritage murals and old godowns. Boats depart from Quayside. Book at the jetty; no reservation needed.
Activity
8:00 pm
Capitol Satay: Satay Celup 🍢
Melaka's obsession: skewers of tofu, prawns, fishcake and offal dunked into a communal bubbling peanut satay broth. Capitol Satay at 41 Lorong Bukit Cina is the original. Order extra lontong rice cakes.
Dinner
Capitol Satay · 41 Lorong Bukit Cina, Melaka
9:30 pm
Jonker Night Market: Street Food Crawl 🌃
Friday night and the market is in full swing. Hit Jonker 88 for the city's most famous Cendol (coconut milk, palm sugar, green pandan jelly), grab grilled Otak-Otak fish cakes from the roadside, and finish with a crispy Apam Balik peanut turnover pancake.
Street Food
Jonker Walk Night Market · Every Friday & Saturday

Street Food Trail · Melaka

Friday · 3 April 2026

Specialty Coffee,
Temples & Street Food Deep Dive

The most food-dense day. Start with a pour-over in a Peranakan courtyard cafe, temple-hop through three faiths on one street, then spend the afternoon eating your way through Melaka's hawker legends.

☀️
Morning
8:00 am
Backlane Coffee ☕
A standout specialty cafe tucked in a restored Peranakan shophouse off Jalan Hang Lekir. Clean, minimalist interior with terrazzo floors and exposed brick. Their single-origin pour-over and cold brew are among the best in Melaka. Pairs perfectly with their Nyonya-spiced banana cake.
Specialty Coffee
Backlane Coffee · Jalan Hang Lekir, Jonker Street
9:30 am
Temple Street: Three Faiths, One Walk
Walk Harmony Street (Jalan Tukang Emas): Cheng Hoon Teng Chinese temple (1646, Malaysia's oldest), Sri Poyyatha Vinayagar Hindu temple (oldest in Malaysia), and Kampung Kling Mosque with its extraordinary Sumatran-Victorian minaret, all within 100 metres of each other.
Culture
11:00 am
Popiah & Tau Fu Fa: Morning Snacks 🥟
Stop at the Popiah stall on Jalan Hang Jebat for fresh spring rolls stuffed with braised jicama, egg and prawns, assembled right in front of you. Then hunt down a Tau Fu Fa (silken tofu dessert) uncle for a warm bowl with gula melaka syrup.
Street Snacks
🌤️
Afternoon
12:30 pm
Low Yong Mow: Legendary Dim Sum 🥢
Since 1948. This legendary kopitiam on Jalan Hang Lekir serves the most characterful dim sum in Melaka: har gao, siu mai and char siu bao steamed in bamboo. No frills, fluorescent lights, packed tables. Perfection.
Dim Sum Lunch
2:00 pm
A Famosa & Porta de Santiago
The 1511 Portuguese fortress gate, survivor of centuries of invasion and near-demolition by the British. A profound landmark. Pause at the gate and try to imagine the fleet that once anchored below.
History
3:00 pm
Menara Taming Sari: 360° Views
Board the revolving gyrotower that lifts you 80 metres for a full panorama of Melaka: the river, the minarets, the red-roofed Dutch quarter, and the Straits beyond. Right on the waterfront, a short walk from A Famosa.
Views
4:00 pm
Anteroom Cafe ☕
A cool, art-forward cafe in a restored colonial building near Jalan Laksamana. Exposed concrete, trailing plants, and a serious espresso menu. The iced Kopi Cendol latte (espresso over cendol jelly and condensed milk) is a Melaka original you won't find elsewhere.
Cool Cafe
5:00 pm
Bukit China — Hilltop Wander
The world's largest Chinese cemetery outside China. A quiet, contemplative climb among century-old Ming tombs with birdsong and sweeping city views. A surprisingly moving detour.
Heritage
🌙
Evening
7:00 pm
Nancy's Kitchen: Peranakan Fine Dining 🍽️
Melaka's most celebrated Nyonya restaurant. Order Ayam Pongteh (chicken braised in fermented soybean and palm sugar), Otak-Otak grilled in banana leaf, and the extraordinary Babi Ponteh pork belly. Recipes passed through generations. Book ahead.
Peranakan Dinner
Nancy's Kitchen · Ayam Pongteh & Nyonya classics
8:30 pm
Jonker Street Night Dessert 🍨
A dedicated dessert lap: Durian Cendol from Jonker 88, Pandan soft-serve from the ice cream carts, or a bowl of ABC (Ais Batu Campur) shaved ice loaded with attap seeds, red beans, jelly and rose syrup. Walk off the meal under the heritage lanterns.
Street Dessert

Day Three
03
Saturday · 4 April 2026

Morning Kopi,
Museums & One Last Feast

Saturday brings Melaka's best market energy. Start with a coconut shake at the seafront, hit the grand museums, then spend the final afternoon eating and shopping before the bus back to Singapore.

Klebang · Melaka
☀️
Morning
8:00 am
Klebang Original Coconut Shake 🥥
A rite of passage. Take a Grab (~15 min from the heritage zone) to the original Klebang stall facing the Straits, where young coconuts are split open and blended into a frothy, ice-cold shake, pulp, flesh, husk and all. Pairs with a roti canai from the mamak next door. Nothing better on a 32°C morning.
Iconic Drink
Klebang Original · Jalan Klebang Besar, facing the Straits
9:30 am
The Geographer Cafe ☕
One of Melaka's most storied cafes, inside a 1930s Dutch-era building beside Christ Church. Order the old-school Kopi-C (coffee with evaporated milk) and a slice of Nyonya pandan cake while watching the square come alive with Saturday morning trishaws.
Heritage Cafe
The Geographer Cafe · Dutch Square, beside Christ Church
10:30 am
Melaka Sultanate Palace Museum
A stunning nail-free wooden replica of the 15th-century Malay palace. Houses treasures from the golden age of the Melaka Sultanate, including royal regalia and ancient maps of the maritime trading routes.
Museum
11:30 am
Maritime Museum: Flora de la Mar
Housed inside a full-scale replica of the Portuguese galleon that sank with Melaka's treasury in 1511. Three atmospheric decks of seafaring history, maps and colonial-era artefacts.
Museum
🌤️
Afternoon
1:00 pm
Ole Sayang: Farewell Peranakan Feast 🍽️
A final, lingering Nyonya lunch: Ayam Pongteh (chicken in fermented soy and coconut palm sugar), Inchi Kabin crispy fried chicken with lime rind, and Chap Chai braised vegetables. One of Melaka's most beloved family restaurants for 30+ years.
Farewell Lunch
2:30 pm
Baba Charlie Nyonya Cake 🎂
The most famous Nyonya cake shop in Melaka on Jalan Hang Jebat. Pack boxes of Onde-onde, Kueh Lapis layered rice cake, Ang Ku Kueh (red tortoise cake) and Kueh Dadar pandan crepes, all made fresh every morning. The best edible souvenirs in the city.
Nyonya Kueh
3:30 pm
Last Souvenir Run
Sweep Jonker Street for the essentials: Nyonya keropok crackers, Dodol coconut toffee blocks, Asam Boi plum candy, hand-stamped batik, and Nyonya kasut beaded shoes. The morning Saturday crowd thins by 3pm.
Shopping
4:30 pm
Departure: Until Next Time
Head back to Singapore with a box of kueh, a bag of dodol, and the particular warmth that Melaka leaves behind. The city has a way of pulling people back, and it will.
Depart

Before You Pack

Things to Bring

☀️  Clothing
  • Lightweight, breathable fabrics (31°C+ and very humid)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for uneven heritage streets
  • Light cardigan or layer for air-conditioned cafes
  • Sunglasses and a brimmed hat or cap
  • A fresh change of clothes for the evening
🌂  Skin & Health
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ (reapply every two hours)
  • Foldable travel umbrella (afternoon showers are quick but heavy)
  • Insect repellent for outdoor hawker areas
  • Antacids or Eno (for ambitious eating days)
  • Small personal first-aid kit
💰  Practical
  • Cash in Malaysian Ringgit (many stalls are cash-only)
  • Reusable tote bag for kueh, dodol and souvenir shopping
  • A charged power bank
  • Grab app set up and ready to use
  • Google Maps downloaded offline for the heritage zone
📷  Photos & Extras
  • Camera or phone with storage cleared
  • Portable charger or extra battery pack
  • Water bottle (stay hydrated in the heat)
  • A genuine sense of curiosity
  • An empty stomach at all times
Plan Ahead

Before You Arrive

🚌
Getting There
Bus from Singapore
~4 hours · departs from Lavender Street or Queen Street Terminal. Operators include Starmart, Transnasional and Konsortium. Book ahead for weekend travel.
🛺
Getting Around
Walk · Grab · Trishaw
The heritage zone is compact and walkable. Hail a decorated trishaw for the full experience. Grab is available for longer hops.
💱
Currency
Malaysian Ringgit (MYR)
Many heritage stalls are cash-only. ATMs are available near Dataran Pahlawan.
🌡️
April Weather
30–33°C · Humid
Pack light breathable clothing, sunscreen, and a small umbrella. Afternoon showers are brief but possible.
🗣️
Language
Bahasa Malaysia
English is widely spoken throughout the heritage and tourist zones. Mandarin and Hokkien are common in heritage shophouses.
🌙
Best Time
Weekends for Night Market
Jonker Walk Night Market runs Friday–Sunday evenings. Avoid sightseeing between 12–3pm on hot days. April 2–4 lands on Thu–Sat, perfect timing.
