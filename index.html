Jonker Walk: The Beating Heart of Heritage Melaka
01
Thursday · 2 April 2026

First Coffee,
Heritage & Hawkers

Roll into Melaka after a 4-hour bus ride from Singapore. Lunch first at the legendary Chung Wah before checking in, then a slow drift through Dutch squares, Peranakan museums, and colonial ruins as the city reveals itself.

08:30 — Morning

Singapore to Melaka

Depart Serangoon Nex MRT Exit E at 8:30 AM on Cityline-Nice To U Express. Direct to Hatten Hotel. ~4 hours. Seats: 25, 26, 27, 28, 31, 32.

12:30 PM
Chung Wah: Chicken Rice Balls

Melaka's most iconic dish — Hainanese chicken hand-rolled into golf-ball orbs. Since the 1960s on Jalan Hang Jebat.

2:30 PM
Check in: Hatten Hotel

Hatten Square, Jalan Merdeka. Steps from Christ Church and the Melaka River.

3:30 — Afternoon

Daily Fix Café

Melaka's most beloved heritage cafe in a restored Peranakan shophouse on Jalan Hang Jebat. Specialty espresso, eggs Benedict on Nyonya toast, housemade kaya jam.

4:30 PM
Baba & Nyonya Heritage Museum

A preserved Peranakan townhouse at 48–50 Jalan Tun Tan Cheng Lock. Porcelain, carved furniture, family heirlooms.

5:30 PM
Christ Church & Dutch Square

The iconic blood-red Dutch colonial square. Golden hour light here is extraordinary.

6:15 PM
St. Paul's Hill at Dusk

Portuguese church ruins of 1521. City-wide views as Melaka lights up across the Straits.

6:30 — Evening

Sunset River Cruise

A 30-minute glide along the Melaka River past painted heritage murals and old godowns. Boats depart from Quayside; no reservation needed.

8:00 PM
Capitol Satay Celup

Skewers dunked in bubbling peanut satay broth. 41 Lorong Bukit Cina. The original, since forever.

9:30 PM
Jonker Night Market

Friday night in full swing. Cendol at Jonker 88, grilled Otak-Otak, crispy Apam Balik pancake.

Specialty Coffee,
Temples & Street Food

02

The most food-dense day. Start with a pour-over in a Peranakan courtyard, temple-hop through three faiths on one street, then spend the afternoon eating through Melaka's hawker legends.

8:00 AM

Backlane Coffee

Specialty cafe in a restored Peranakan shophouse off Jalan Hang Lekir. Single-origin pour-over, cold brew, Nyonya-spiced banana cake.

9:30 AM

Temple Street

Harmony Street: Cheng Hoon Teng (1646), Sri Poyyatha Hindu Temple, and Kampung Kling Mosque — all within 100 metres.

11:00 AM

Popiah & Tau Fu Fa

Fresh spring rolls with braised jicama + silken tofu with gula melaka syrup. Morning street snacks done right.

12:30 PM

Low Yong Mow Dim Sum

Since 1948. Har gao, siu mai, char siu bao. Fluorescent lights, packed tables. Jalan Hang Lekir. Perfection.

2:00 PM

A Famosa & Porta de Santiago

The 1511 Portuguese fortress gate. Survivor of centuries of invasion. One of Southeast Asia's most profound landmarks.

3:00 PM

Menara Taming Sari

Revolving gyrotower, 80 metres up. Full panorama: river, minarets, red Dutch quarter, Straits beyond.

4:00 PM

Anteroom Café

Art-forward cafe in a restored colonial building near Jalan Laksamana. Iced Kopi Cendol latte is a Melaka original.

5:00 PM

Bukit China

World's largest Chinese cemetery outside China. Century-old Ming tombs, birdsong, sweeping city views.

Evening Feature
7:00 PM

Nancy's Kitchen: Peranakan Fine Dining

Melaka's most celebrated Nyonya restaurant. Ayam Pongteh (chicken in fermented soybean and palm sugar), Otak-Otak grilled in banana leaf, Babi Ponteh pork belly. Recipes passed through generations. Book ahead.

8:30 PM
Jonker Street Night Dessert

Durian Cendol at Jonker 88, Pandan soft-serve, or ABC shaved ice loaded with attap seeds and rose syrup.

Before You Pack

Things to Bring

Clothing
  • Lightweight, breathable fabrics (31°C+ and very humid)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for uneven heritage streets
  • Light cardigan for air-conditioned cafes
  • Sunglasses and a brimmed hat or cap
  • A fresh change of clothes for the evening
Skin & Health
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ (reapply every 2 hours)
  • Foldable umbrella (afternoon showers are quick but heavy)
  • Insect repellent for outdoor hawker areas
  • Antacids or Eno (for ambitious eating days)
  • Small personal first-aid kit
Practical
  • Cash in Malaysian Ringgit (many stalls are cash-only)
  • Reusable tote bag for kueh, dodol and souvenirs
  • A charged power bank
  • Grab app set up and ready
  • Google Maps downloaded offline
Photos & Extras
  • Camera or phone with storage cleared
  • Portable charger or extra battery pack
  • Water bottle (stay hydrated in the heat)
  • A genuine sense of curiosity
  • An empty stomach at all times
Plan Ahead

Before You Arrive

Getting There
Bus from Singapore

Departs Serangoon Nex MRT Exit E at 8:30 AM (Cityline-Nice To U Express) · arrives Hatten Hotel directly · ~4 hours. Return: 707 Inc from Hub 28 at 12:10 PM to Woodlands.

Getting Around
Walk · Grab · Trishaw

The heritage zone is compact and walkable. Hail a decorated trishaw for the full experience. Grab is available for longer hops.

Currency
Malaysian Ringgit (MYR)

Many heritage stalls are cash-only. ATMs are available near Dataran Pahlawan.

April Weather
30–33°C · Humid

Pack light breathable clothing, sunscreen, and a small umbrella. Afternoon showers are brief but possible.

Language
Bahasa Malaysia

English is widely spoken throughout the heritage and tourist zones. Mandarin and Hokkien are common in heritage shophouses.

Best Time
Weekends for Night Market

Jonker Walk Night Market runs Friday–Sunday evenings. April 2–4 lands on Thu–Sat — perfect timing.
